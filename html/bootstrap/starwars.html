EPISODE IV
A NEW HOPE FOR IPv4

The critical question is should we upgrade to IPv6. It appears to be our only hope.

The resistance has been working on a new technology called Enhanced IP.

It works well with IPv4. In fact, it's addresses are two IP addresses concatenated together like so: 192.0.2.2.10.0.0.1.

A challenge for the resistance is to get the core of the Internet to pass certain packets containing IP options. The evil 'sixth' might try to stop this from happening. Fortunately, there is a plan in case this happens.

What does Enhanced IP work with? Samba, http, ssh, and more.

How many addresses does Enhanced IP support? Approximately 2^56.

Is Enhanced IP an extension to NAT? Yes. It adds the possibility of end-to-end addressesing back in.

What is the draw to Enhanced IP? The transition plan is much simpler than IPv6 and it might just work on a global scale.

What does Enhanced IP need? You.

Specifically what does Enhanced IP need? We need kernel developers, sysadmins, testers, and people who want to drop us a line to show their support.
